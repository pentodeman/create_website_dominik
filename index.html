About me

I'm a junior at Texas Tech University majoring in Computer Engineering. This area of study is perfect for me as it combines hardware design and fabrication with low level software and firmware development. I tend to focus on the hardware side of things, but I am also competent in software development in languages like C and C++, Python, and Assembly for embedded devices. I am also good at FPGA development and prototyping in Verilog.

During my time at Tech, I've maintained a 4.0 GPA while completing school projects, and most importantly, the personal ones. My main interest is circuit design. I focus on analog video and audio processing, but I also design digital electronics. My most recent project is a VGA frontend circuit capable of processing and amplifying color analog video, detecting sync polarity, and driving XY displays by generating a raster scan synchronized with the video signal. I've also recently designed a dynamic microphone preamplifier for my Rode PodMic designed to be mounted on my DIY microphone arm that attaches to my monitor mount with 3D-printed hardware. My most recent digital project is a macro keypad designed to natively emulate keystrokes and print text as an HID device. I'm planning to implement shortcuts and key combinations in the code and write a Linux kernel driver that would handle displaying feedback information as a screen overlay when cycling through the 15 text and symbol presets. I also made an automated water pump for my 5 gallon water jug that has had an uptime of over a year and prevented many spills while supplying my high water demand of gallons per week. It detects the presence of a water bottle under its outlet and dispenses the set amount with one press of a button. While dispensing water, it measures the dispensed amount using a strain gauge. It implements numerous safety checks to only pour into an empty container. This has by far been my most used DIY tool. I also enjoy truck simulators in VR as a hobby, and for it I made a custom shifter knob with split and range selectors that interacts with the game as a gamepad. This allows me to shift trucks as it is done in real life in a truck with a manual transmission up to 16 gears. My most notable programming project is a video stream scraper written in Python that I've maintained for over 5 years now. It is a tool made purely for fun to learn about video streaming techniques and how they are implemented in network. Disclaimer: this project is only used educationally, in a white hat fashion on my own, self-hosted video streams on a local server that implements common streaming protocols. This project also taught me a lot about threaded code, subprocesses, and GUI programming using the QT6 toolkit. Another project I enjoyed writing was a simple schedule display program that displays my school schedule in an asthetically pleasing CLI table. I wrote it in object-oriented C++ while taking my OOP class to experience OOP in a real language. Yes, I'm not a fan of Java. Today I'll be working on implementing a schedule builder for this project to avoid writing schedule files with custom formatting by hand.

I spend most of my time working on electronics, programming, and CAD projects. I usually combine all of those skills in my projects to create different, more or less useful devices. Outside of that, I also enjoy customizing and streamlining my computing experience. Of course, all of my computers run Linux by default with only my desktop being allowed having a Windows drive for occasional light gaming. As required by the distro I use, I must say that "I use Arch btw." I enjoy customizing my environment as much as possible. My current setup consists of Arch Linux (6.12.8-arch1-1 kernel) with an i3 tiling window manager running on X11. My favorite terminal emulator is Kitty, though my friends seem to disagree with my life choices. For CAD, I prefer not to boot into Windows as my computer takes around 1.5 minutes to boot due to my memory size and type, so I opt to run it in a Windows virtual machine with hardware acceleration. In the past, I used to run it it a VM with single GPU passthrough, but this approach was not very stable due to the GPU I use and my IOMMU grouping. I am currently considering buying a second, low power workstation GPU for testing.
